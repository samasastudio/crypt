# T.J. Miracle Diet — Document Read & Analysis

**Source:** Typewritten document attributed to Sacred Heart Hospital, Spokane, WA  
**Claim:** Used for overweight heart patients. Guaranteed to lose 10–17 pounds in one week.

---

## Original Document Transcription

### Daily Schedule

| Day | Rules |
|---|---|
| **Day 1 (Monday)** | All fruit you want, all day — except bananas. Melons especially recommended. |
| **Day 2 (Tuesday)** | All vegetables — fresh, raw or cooked. No beans, peas, or corn. Eat a large baked potato with butter. |
| **Day 3 (Wednesday)** | All fruit and vegetables — no potato or bananas. |
| **Day 4 (Thursday)** | Eat 8 bananas and drink 8 glasses of skim milk with all the TJ Soup you want. |
| **Day 5 (Friday)** | Beef and vegetables. Eat 10–20 oz of beef and tomatoes and a medium potato. |
| **Day 6 (Saturday)** | Beef and vegetables. All you want, no potato. |
| **Day 7 (Sunday)** | Brown rice, fruit juice (unsweetened), and vegetables. |

> **Core rule:** Continue in this way in order until you have reached the weight you want. Keep the days in order — no switching around, no substitutions. Eat all the TJ Soup you want.

---

### TJ Soup Recipe

**Ingredients:**
- 1 large onion
- 1 head cabbage
- 2 or 3 green peppers
- 1 large stalk of celery
- Tomatoes — fresh, canned, or juice (1 large can)
- 2 pkgs dry onion soup mix (2 pkgs are in 1 box)

**Instructions:** Cut vegetables and cover with water in a large pot. Boil 10 min. Cover and lower heat. Simmer veggies until soft. Eat TJ Soup anytime, as much as you want and as often as you like. There are no calories in this soup. The more you eat the more you will lose.

## Analysis

### What This Diet Is

The T.J. Miracle Diet (also widely known as the **Sacred Heart Diet** or **Cabbage Soup Diet**) is a folk crash diet that has circulated since at least the 1980s. Despite its hospital attribution, no verified connection to Sacred Heart Hospital in Spokane, WA has ever been established. It appears in typewritten, photocopied, and printed form across multiple decades with minor variations — a hallmark of chain-letter style health content.

It is **not a medically supervised protocol.**

### How It Works (Mechanically)

The diet produces rapid short-term weight loss through three primary mechanisms:

1. **Severe caloric restriction** — most days are extremely low calorie. Fruit-only and vegetable-only days with unlimited soup likely land between 600–1,000 kcal/day for most people.
2. **Water weight loss** — low sodium intake combined with diuretic vegetables (cabbage, celery, onion) causes rapid fluid excretion, which accounts for the majority of the 10–17 lb claimed loss.
3. **Glycogen depletion** — reduced carbohydrate intake (especially Days 1–3) causes the body to burn through glycogen stores. Each gram of glycogen holds ~3g of water, so depletion = rapid scale movement with minimal actual fat loss.

### Nutritional Profile by Day

| Day | Protein | Carbs | Fat | Notable Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Very low | Moderate (fruit sugars) | Very low | Protein, fat |
| Tue | Low | Moderate | Low (butter) | Protein |
| Wed | Very low | Moderate | Very low | Protein, fat |
| Thu | Moderate (milk) | High (bananas) | Low | Variety, micronutrients |
| Fri | High (beef) | Moderate | Moderate | Fiber, variety |
| Sat | High (beef) | Low | Moderate | Carbs, fiber |
| Sun | Low | High (rice) | Very low | Protein, fat |

### Strengths
- Forces high vegetable and fiber intake on most days
- Eliminates processed foods, alcohol, and added sugars entirely
- The soup provides a genuinely nutritious base (cabbage, celery, tomatoes, onion)
- Short duration (7 days) limits risk of sustained nutritional deficiency
- Thursday banana/milk day provides potassium and calcium that most crash diets lack

### Weaknesses
- **Thursday is nutritionally bizarre** — 8 bananas + skim milk with no other food is heavy on simple carbs and light on everything else
- **Protein is severely inadequate** on Days 1, 2, 3, and 7 — muscle loss is likely over repeated cycles
- **No healthy fats** on most days — essential fatty acid intake is near zero Mon–Wed
- **The 10–17 lb claim is misleading** — the majority is water weight that returns within days of resuming normal eating
- **"No calories in this soup" is factually incorrect** — the soup has calories; it's just very low calorie
- **Not sustainable** — repeating cycles risks nutrient deficiency, metabolic adaptation, and disordered eating patterns

### Verdict

As a one-week reset or kickstart, the diet is low-risk for healthy adults and will produce noticeable scale results, primarily from water weight and glycogen. It won't meaningfully impact body fat in a single week. The vegetable-heavy structure is genuinely good for gut health and micronutrient intake. The banana-milk day and the near-zero protein on fruit/veggie days are its weakest points nutritionally.

**Best use case:** A one-time week-long cleanse to reset eating habits, reduce bloat, and build momentum — not a repeated or extended protocol.
